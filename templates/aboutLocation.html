SmartAttendance uses geolocation to verify that a student is physically present within the class area when marking attendance. This prevents proxy attendance and ensures fairness for all.

üåê How It Works

When a student tries to mark attendance, the system fetches their current GPS coordinates from the browser or device. It compares this location with the instructor-defined radius (e.g., 100 meters around the classroom). If the student is within the zone, attendance is allowed.

Location data is never stored permanently. It is used only at the moment of attendance marking. Students are always informed before location access is requested, maintaining full transparency.
